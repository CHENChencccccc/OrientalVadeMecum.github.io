Tourism Regions of Lhasa

Barkhor Street: This is the center of the old town and a must-visit place for tourists. Barkhor Street is a bustling marketplace where you can find traditional Tibetan handicrafts, souvenirs, and local food.

Lhasa River Valley: Located on the outskirts of the city, this scenic area offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and a chance to explore the traditional Tibetan way of life.

Tourist Attractions of Lhasa

1. Potala Palace: This iconic palace is one of the most famous landmarks in Lhasa. It was once the residence of the Dalai Lama and is now a museum showcasing Tibetan culture and history.

2. Jokhang Temple: This ancient temple is the spiritual heart of Lhasa and an important pilgrimage site for Tibetan Buddhists. The temple is known for its unique architectural style and beautiful interior.

3. Norbulingka Palace: This beautiful palace was once the summer residence of the Dalai Lama. It is famous for its stunning gardens and unique Tibetan architecture.

4. Sera Monastery: This is one of the three largest monasteries in Lhasa and is known for its lively debates between monks.

Local Cuisine of Lhasa

1. Tibetan Butter Tea: This traditional drink is made with yak butter, tea leaves, and salt. It's a staple of Tibetan cuisine and is often served as a welcome drink for visitors.

2. Tsampa: This is a staple food in Tibet and is made from roasted barley flour. It can be served in various forms, including as a porridge or mixed with butter tea.

3. Momos: These are Tibetan dumplings filled with meat or vegetables. They are a popular snack and can be found in many street food stalls in Lhasa.

4. Yak Meat: Yak meat is a common ingredient in Tibetan cuisine and can be found in many local dishes, including stews and curries.
