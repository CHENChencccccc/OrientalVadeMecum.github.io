Dali Tourism

Dali is a popular tourist destination located in the Yunnan Province of China. It is renowned for its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine.In this travel guide, we will provide an overview of the different regions to visit in Dali, the top tourist attractions, and the must-try local dishes. Dali is divided into two main regions, the Dali Ancient City and the Cangshan Mountain Scenic Area. The Ancient City is the cultural heart of Dali and is famous for its well-preserved ancient buildings and traditional Bai culture. On the other hand, the Cangshan Mountain Scenic Area is a natural paradise with picturesque mountains, tranquil lakes, and refreshing hot springs. Three Pagodas of Chongsheng Temple - The Three Pagodas are an iconic symbol of Dali and one of the most significant Buddhist monuments in China. Erhai Lake - Erhai Lake is a crystal-clear freshwater lake surrounded by rolling green hills and quaint fishing villages. It's the perfect place for a leisurely boat ride.

Guilin's Tourism

Guilin is a scenic city located in the northeast of Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, China.It is famous for its picturesque natural scenery, unique karst landscape, and rich cultural heritage.Visitors can enjoy exploring the city's numerous tourist areas, visiting famous attractions, and trying the delicious local cuisine.1. Elephant Trunk Hill: This landmark is Guilin's most famous natural landmark, resembling an elephant drinking water from the river.2. Reed Flute Cave: This natural limestone cave features beautiful rock formations illuminated by colorful lights, making for a magical sightseeing experience.3. Seven Star Park: This beautiful park features gorgeous natural scenery and historic landmarks, including the Seven Star Cave, the Flower Bridge, and the Camel Hill.4. Fubo Hill: This hill is a popular destination for its stunning panoramic views of Guilin and the Li River.
