Travel Stragegy

Day 1: Arrive in Xian and check into your hotel. Visit the famous Terracotta Warriors and Horses Museum, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular attractions in Xian Explore the surrounding area and try some local street food

Day 2: Start your day by walking or biking along the ancient city walls of Xian, which date back to the Ming Dynasty and offer beautiful views of the city. After exploring the walls, visit the Shaanxi History Museum, which showcases the history and culture of Shaanxi province and includes many ancient artifacts and relics. For lunch, head back to the Muslim Quarter and try some more local street food. In the afternoon, visit the Big Wild Goose Pagoda, a Buddhist pagoda built in the Tang Dynasty that houses a large collection of Buddhist scriptures. End your day by watching the Tang Dynasty Music and Dance Show, a spectacular performance that showcases traditional Chinese music and dance.

Day 3: Spend the morning exploring the Shaanxi History Museum, which showcases the history and culture of Shaanxi province and includes many ancient artifacts and relics. In the afternoon, take a stroll through the beautiful Tang Paradise, a theme park that recreates the grandeur and elegance of the Tang Dynasty. Be sure to catch the water and light show in the evening. End your last night in Xian with a hot pot dinner, which is a popular and delicious way to enjoy a meal with friends and family.

Food Recommentation

1.Roujiamo (Chinese-style hamburger) - This is a popular street food in Xian, consisting of a meat filling (usually pork or beef) stuffed inside a freshly baked bun. The meat is typically slow-cooked and seasoned with spices, making it flavorful and juicy.

2. Yangrou paomo (lamb stew with bread) - This is a hearty and filling dish that is popular in Xian. It consists of tender chunks of lamb, vegetables, and small pieces of unleavened bread that are added to the soup to soak up the flavors.

3. Jiasan guan tangbao (steamed soup dumplings) - These dumplings are filled with a savory soup and meat filling, and are a must-try when visiting Xian. They are typically served with a vinegar and ginger dipping sauce.

4. Steam Pot Chicken:This is a traditional Yunnan dish that involves steaming chicken in a pot with various spices, such as ginger, garlic, and chili. It is served with dipping sauce made from salt, pepper, and sesame oil.

5. Pita bread soaked in lamb soup - This is a traditional dish in Xian that consists of a freshly baked pita bread that is cut into pieces and then soaked in a flavorful lamb soup. It is a comforting and warming dish, perfect for a cold day.
