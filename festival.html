Festivals in CHINA

New Year's Day:New Year's Day is the first day of the calendar year, typically observed on January 1st. It is a public holiday in many countries around the world, and is often celebrated with fireworks, parties, and other festivities.

The Spring Festival:The Spring Festival is a time for family reunions, and many people travel long distances to be with their loved ones. It is a time for celebrating the new year, paying respect to ancestors, and exchanging gifts. During the festival, people decorate their homes with red lanterns, couplets, and other traditional decorations.

The Lantern Festival:The Lantern Festival is a traditional Chinese festival that marks the end of the Chinese New Year celebrations. It usually falls on the 15th day of the first lunar month, which is around mid-February to mid-March on the Gregorian calendar.

Mid-Autumn Festival:The Mid-Autumn Festival, also known as the Moon Festival, is a traditional Chinese festival celebrated on the 15th day of the eighth lunar month, which usually falls in September or October on the Gregorian calendar.

Events in CHINA

1.Spring Festival (Chinese New Year): This is the most important festival in China, and it marks the beginning of the lunar new year. Celebrations last for 15 days, and they include fireworks, dragon and lion dances, family gatherings, and the exchange of red envelopes filled with money.

2.Dragon Boat Festival: This festival is celebrated on the fifth day of the fifth lunar month, and it is marked by dragon boat races, traditional foods such as zongzi (sticky rice dumplings), and the hanging of calamus and wormwood leaves to repel evil spirits.

3.Mid-Autumn Festival: This festival is celebrated on the 15th day of the eighth lunar month, and it is a day for family gatherings, moon watching, and the sharing of mooncakes.

4.Laba Festival: This festival is celebrated on the eighth day of the twelfth lunar month, and it is a day for offering sacrifices to ancestors and gods, as well as enjoying Laba porridge, a traditional dish made from mixed grains and beans.
