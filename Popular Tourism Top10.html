TOP3 Lhasa

Lhasa is the capital of the Tibet Autonomous Region, located in the southwest of China. As the center of Tibetan culture, Lhasa is known for its stunning high-altitude landscapes, ancient temples, and unique traditions. The city is situated at an elevation of over 3,600 meters above sea level, making it one of the highest cities in the world. Lhasa is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the fascinating culture and history of Tibet.

TOP4 Chengdu

Chengdu is located in the western part of China and is the capital city of Sichuan Province. It is an important transportation hub and cultural center of China. The city is surrounded by mountains and rivers, with a mild climate and a rich cultural heritage. The region has a number of popular tourist destinations, including the city center, the outskirts of the city, and the surrounding mountains and national parks.

TOP5 Xi'an

Xi'an is one of the most historically and culturally significant cities in China, and a popular destination for travelers seeking to explore China's ancient past. The city has served as the capital of multiple dynasties throughout Chinese history and is known for its many historical sites, including the Terracotta Warriors and Horses, the city walls, and the Shaanxi History Museum. In addition to its rich history, Xi'an is also famous for its delicious cuisine, which includes famous dishes like roujiamo (a kind of sandwich) and biangbiang noodles.

TOP10 Sanya
