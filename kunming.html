Humanities in Shanghai

Wukang Road: Those who cannot write poetry want to write poetry, those who cannot draw want to draw, and those who cannot sing want to sing. It feels wonderful. Many historical celebrities' former residences, century old buildings, and endless shops have made Wukang Road a must-have stop for travel on the train.

Changle Road: a road that combines fashion, romance, and nostalgia. Qian Zhongshu and Zhang Ailing have also left their stories here, and Changle Road is also my favorite road on this trip. It used to be the birthplace of the Chinese fashion brand. Now, there are many characteristic literary and art shops along the road. There are some beautiful old western-style houses along the way. People living here must be very happy.

Julu Road: In the daytime, this place is filled with inconspicuous garden houses and market houses. When night falls, Julu Road takes on a unique look, providing a great experience of Shanghai's bar and nightclub culture.

Travel Stragegy

Day 1: Start your day with a visit to the Green Lake Park, a popular park with locals and tourists alike. Take a leisurely walk around the lake, enjoy the scenic views and watch people practicing Tai Chi or dancing. Head to the Yunnan Provincial Museum, which offers a glimpse into the province's rich history and culture. The museum has several exhibitions on the diverse ethnic groups in Yunnan and displays artifacts from the region's ancient past. In the evening, visit the Kunming Night Market, a lively street market with an array of local snacks, street food, and souvenirs. Try some of the local delicacies such as the rice noodles and Yunnan-style BBQ.

Day 2: Take a day trip to the Stone Forest, a UNESCO World Heritage site located about 90 km from Kunming. The Stone Forest is a unique limestone formation that has been eroded over millions of years, creating a forest-like landscape of towering rocks and pinnacles. On your way back from the Stone Forest, stop by the Bai Minority Village, a traditional Bai village that showcases their unique architecture, culture, and customs. Take a stroll around the village and visit some of the local workshops to see how Bai people make their traditional handicrafts.

Day 3: Start your day with a visit to the Golden Temple, a Buddhist temple located on the Mingfeng Hill. The temple is famous for its large copper temple building, which is covered in gold leaf and shines brightly in the sunlight. Visit the Yuantong Temple, one of the oldest and most important temples in Kunming. The temple is known for its beautiful architecture, tranquil atmosphere, and large collection of Buddhist statues and relics. End your day by visiting the Western Hills, a scenic mountain range located just outside of Kunming. Take a cable car ride to the top of the mountain to enjoy panoramic views of Kunming and the Dianchi Lake. You can also visit the Dragon Gate, a series of caves and temples carved into the side of the mountain.

Food Recommentation

1.Across-the-bridge noodles:This is a popular dish in Yunnan Province, and the most famous dish in Kunming. It is a noodle soup dish with chicken broth, vegetables, meat slices, and raw egg yolk.

2. Yunnan-style hot pot:Yunnan-style hot pot is different from other hot pot styles in China. The soup base is made with fresh herbs, spices, and Yunnan ham. It is served with various vegetables, meats, and local mushrooms, which are believed to have medicinal properties.

3. Baba:Baba is a traditional flatbread made from cornmeal, flour, and water. It is usually served with pickled vegetables or Yunnan ham.

4. Steam Pot Chicken:This is a traditional Yunnan dish that involves steaming chicken in a pot with various spices, such as ginger, garlic, and chili. It is served with dipping sauce made from salt, pepper, and sesame oil.

5. Yunnan-style BBQ:Yunnan-style BBQ features marinated meats grilled over charcoal, and usually served with dipping sauce made from sesame paste and spices.
